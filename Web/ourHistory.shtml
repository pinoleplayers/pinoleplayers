Our History

In 1986, two Pinole (California) couples, Gary & Nancy Gill and David & Patti Clark met around the Gill's kitchen table to discuss their dream of forming a local community theatre group. Utilizing Gary's technical knowledge, Nancy's musical talents, David's directing background and Patti's producing and organizational skills, the PINOLE COMMUNITY PLAYERS were born. Contact was made with Father Tom Ryan at  St. Joseph's Church to seek support for the new endeavor. With Father Ryan's blessing and financial support, and the use of the school auditorium, GODSPELL, the first production of the new theatrical company, was born. Tryout's were conducted and an outstanding cast of young local people hit the newly constructed stage in July of 1986. Two more successful seasons followed with the productions of OLIVER, THE FANTASTICKS and SOUTH PACIFIC, but in 1989 the St. Joseph's auditorium was no longer available and it became necessary to find a new venue for the Pinole Community Players.

At this point, the City of Pinole offered the use of the Pinole Memorial Center. Able to pack up lights, scenery and costumes at a moments notice, the Pinole Players became a band of traveling performers in the true theatrical tradition. COMPANY, A CHORUS LINE, BABY, ANNIE, A LITTLE SHOP OF HORRORS and GUYS & DOLLS brought PCP to their 1991-92 SEASON – but still homeless. But by now, the Pinole Community Players consisted of many talented local people, including entire families -- all bitten by the theatrical bug -- who worked diligently and tirelessly to make PCP a well-respected performance group. In 1990, the Board of Directors approached the Pinole City Council with the possibility of leasing the Pinole Memorial Center as the Players' permanent performance space.

By the summer of 1991, the City Council voted unanimously in favor of this agreement, pledged their support to the group and granted permission to establish the PINOLE COMMUNITY PLAYHOUSE. THE BEST OF BROADWAY opened the 1991-92 season and plans were underway to make renovations to the building and expand the number of adult and children's performances each year. This group has been able to accomplish renovations to the building, an increase in audience attendance, introduction of new directors and new productions. Pinole Community Players' quality productions, intimate performance space, unique cabaret style and family atmosphere continue to be strengths as the second decade begins.

Voted "Best in the West" by West County Times readers and supported by civic leaders, theatre goers, and the community in general.
