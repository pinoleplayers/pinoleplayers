Tennessee Williams'

Winner of the 1955
Pulitzer Prize for Drama

Set in the bed-sitting room of the Mississippi Delta plantation home of Big Daddy Pollitt, a wealthy cotton tycoon, the play examines the relationships among members of Big Daddy's family: his wife Big Mama, his sons Brick and Gooper, their wives, Maggie the "Cat" and Mae. Cat on a Hot Tin Roof examines social mores, greed, superficiality, decay, sexual desire, and death.

Directed by: Brian Katz
